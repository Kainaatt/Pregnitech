 [Common Symptoms]

- Quickening: mother feels fluttering or kicks.
- Visible baby bump; abdominal growth.
- Weight gain 2–3 kg common by month’s midpoint.
- Increased appetite; cravings frequent.
- Heartburn due to relaxed esophageal muscles.
- Constipation may persist; fiber intake helps.
- Swelling of feet, ankles, hands possible.
- Round ligament pain; pulling in lower abdomen.
- Backache from posture changes.
- Leg cramps at night.
- Increased vaginal discharge — normal.
- Stretch marks may appear on abdomen, hips, thighs, breasts.
- Linea nigra visible on abdomen.
- Breasts fuller; nipples darker.
- Skin changes: melasma, oiliness, pregnancy glow.
- Hair growth may accelerate; nails stronger.
- Mood swings usually mild.
- Energy levels higher; morning sickness usually subsides.
- Sleep patterns more regular; vivid dreams common.

- Bloating and gas common.

- Light uterine cramping as uterus expands.
- Libido may increase.

- Some women experience mild varicose veins.
- Mild swelling in evening common.
- Mild fatigue after long activity.
- Emotions generally stabilized compared to first trimester.
- Mother may feel more connected to baby through movements.

 [When to Take a Pregnancy Test]

- Usually already confirmed by Month 5; blood tests can monitor hCG levels.
- Tests at this stage rarely needed unless checking for hormonal anomalies.
- Ultrasound confirmation is standard by this trimester.
- Follow-up tests may check progesterone or other hormone levels if indicated.
- If experiencing bleeding or unusual symptoms, contact healthcare provider rather than retesting.
- Prenatal screenings (quad screen, glucose tests) often performed during Month 5.
- hCG levels stabilize by this month; urine tests less informative.
- Rely on ultrasound and maternal monitoring for fetal health confirmation.



 [FAQ]

- How big is the baby? About 5–6 inches long, weighs 150–200 grams at start of month.
- Can I feel movements? Yes, quickening becomes more distinct.
- Is sex safe? Yes, unless doctor advises otherwise.
- Is exercise allowed? Light to moderate activity recommended; avoid high-impact exercises.
- Why do I have back pain? Uterus growth and posture changes cause strain.
- Are stretch marks normal? Yes, common on abdomen, thighs, breasts.
- Will I gain weight? About 2–3 kg by midpoint; varies individually.
- Can I travel? Generally safe if no complications; follow provider advice.
- Is swelling normal? Mild swelling in hands, feet, or ankles is common.
- Can I dye my hair? Best to wait or use pregnancy-safe products.
- Why am I more emotional? Hormonal changes and maternal adjustment.
- Is heartburn normal? Yes, common due to progesterone relaxing esophageal muscles.
- Should I continue prenatal vitamins? Absolutely, continue folic acid, iron, calcium.
- Can I continue work? Yes, if environment is safe; avoid heavy lifting or stress.
- Why am I more hungry? Increased metabolic demands; eat balanced meals.
- Can I sleep on my back? Limit back sleeping; left side preferred for circulation.
- Are leg cramps normal? Yes, due to circulation and magnesium levels.
- How often should I see the doctor? Typically every 4 weeks in second trimester.
- Can I have sex? Yes, as long as comfortable; avoid if complications arise.
- Will I feel baby hiccups? Yes, rhythmic movements can be noticed.
- Can I continue skincare routine? Yes, if products are pregnancy-safe.
- What about travel vaccines? Discuss with provider if planning international travel.
